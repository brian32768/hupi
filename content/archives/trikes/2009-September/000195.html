On Sunday 13 September 2009, Kate Peterson wrote:
> All -- I have a Greenspeed with Hope Closed 2 disk brakes.  Because of a
> combination of circumstances I have not ridden it since last summer. When I
> got it out today one of the brakes seems to be stuck in the closed
> position.  Opening the screw adjuster has no effect. The brake lever does
> feel a little 'squishy' but it has hardly any play compared to the opposite
> brake and the pads are clearly gripping the disk. While it is possible to
> ride the bike in a straight line (that is the brake is not locked up) the
> wheel barely moves when you lift the front end of the trike and attempt to
> spin the wheel.
>
> The Hope instructions don't cover this situation.  Should I try bleeding
> the brake and replacing the fluid or???? Thanks for any help.
>
Hey,
        I have  Trice Explorer with  Hope Minidiscs.  I have found that they 
are very easy to disassemble, clean and reassemble and bleed.

If the pistons are just stuck through lack of use (as Peter Bradshaw suspects) 
then be carefull as you follow his instructions in case the pistons have 
stuck to the seal .. you dont want to tear the seal.  I got a lot of spares 
for my Hope Minidiscs from:-

   http://www.ukbikestore.co.uk/

Seals, washers, etc

The Hope website is at:-

   http://www.hopetech.com/

There is some technical information on that site. 

One of my discs had an oversize piston which caused it to stick as yours do.
I contacted Hope in the USA and they refurbished the caliper for me.  You 
might wish to contact them if you feel you cannot handle freeing the piston 
yourself.  They repaired my caliper and got it back to me within two days .. 
they were in Southern California (in Cherry Valley) and I live in San jose so 
YMMV.  I dont know if they still have that office in the USA.

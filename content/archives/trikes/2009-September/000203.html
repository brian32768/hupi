Hi Jim, I have some 155mm road cranks in 130/74bcd configuration  
(http://www.pbwbikes.com/product_info.php/cPath/26/products_id/65) that  
are well-made generic ones.

It is tempting to drill a hole and tap it by hand. In practice, it is  
almost impossible to get the hole to be lined up correctly. If the hole is  
drilled or the tapping is akimbo, then your foot will rotate around in a  
randomly oriented circle as you pedal. This may not be good for your knee.

If you want to be sure of it, make sure you hold the crank accurately and  
securely and drill straight down with a machine. A milling machine works  
great if you have some fixturing.

A Schlumpf-drive is easily configured with a wide range of lengths.

Best regards, --Hugh

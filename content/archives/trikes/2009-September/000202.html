Jim: If you are uncertain about whether to spring for shorter cranks 
(or, perhaps more importantly, what length to get), you might consider 
buying a set of crank shorteners. It will save the hassle of finding a 
decent used crank and then having to get the holes tapped correctly.

I got a set from Tandems East [tandemseast at gmail.com]. They cost me 
$115.00 last November for a set that has four holes (they shorten the 
crank arms by 24mm, 41mm, 59mm & 76mm), which gives you a wide set of 
options. They easily bolt onto your current cranks and so far they have 
stayed put. No play, no loosening. I shortened my crank length just one 
notch: from 175mm to 151mm but it made pedaling a lot easier.

FYI.

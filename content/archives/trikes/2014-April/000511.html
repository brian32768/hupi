On 23/04/2014 7:22 AM, Bob Stuart wrote:
> Mea Culpa.  Back in 1980, I'd have bet that there would be more slick 
> magazines full of trike components than model aircraft mags by now.  
> Instead, we are still struggling along with bicycle parts, sometimes 
> putting the rear derailleur cage closer to the ground than the wheel rim.
>
> What I've had on the drawing board for far too long is very different, 
> and need not be expensive to make.  It takes into account the need to 
> guide the chain against side forces, and rationalizes the whole affair 
> for a RWD recumbent tadpole trike. The chain tensioner has always been 
> used to select rear cogs because it fits well there on a bike, but a 
> simple cage, as on the front derailleur is more than adequate for the 
> slack run.  The chain tensioner can be relocated closer to the middle 
> of the run, to replace the idlers often used,  and the moving idler 
> can be just guided in a straight line by a couple of loose slots in 
> the chain guard and a spring.  That provides plenty of take-up to run 
> big, efficient chainrings and super-wide range gears for a 
> streamliner.  Efficiency on the rear cogs falls off badly under 15t, 
> and big front rings reduce the chain force, reducing wear and frame 
> flex.  They even give more room for a Browning type shifter on the 
> front, to work even under power, like the rear.

In your experience, do the higher chain velocities cost anything much? I 
was thinking of friction in guide tubes and things like that. I like big 
sprockets too, but was wondering about all that chain flying around.

Giles.

Sorry, semileading was the correct term, sorry for that.

I think that the progression shows that your are more open minded than I gave you credit for at the beginning. Sorry for that too.

Another good thing with this development is that it shows that you can do a good job with less and lighter and less complicated parts. I guess the geometry is simpler too.

I can see that there is a potential to build this system very compact with narrow track and low CG. It can probably also be built to give good leg clearance in that case.

For instance, for comparison, my velomobile has got a very complicated suspension system that only gives a travel of 35 mm. It is a kind of multilink mcpherson system. The good thing with this system is that the seating position can be very low with good leg clearance and at the same time the wheels are kept snuggly within the bodywork. Seat height is only 15 cm.

/Bruno

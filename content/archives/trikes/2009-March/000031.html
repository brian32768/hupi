I have built a new suspension trike. Like my last design, it uses Firestone
rolling lip airbag springs and a single rear damper. 
 
Front track 90cm, wheelbase 102cm, (loaded) seat height 34cm
 
Front suspension is unique: leading arm, torsion tube, beam axle with Watts
Link. Rear suspension is space-frame trailing arm.
 
7 inches rear travel, 6 inches front travel for single wheel bumps, 4 inches
for simultaneous two wheel bumps.
 
Front kingpins are Greenspeed GT3 mark 2 with their negative scrub radius,
swapped left to right. I run more static castor. Steering is a variation on
Greenspeed 'non-crossover' steering optimised for ratio, Ackermann and bump
steer.
 
Wheels are 16 inch. 
 
81 gears, front drum brakes, rear hydraulic disc brake. 
 
All chrome moly steel, nickel bronze brazed together. 
 
21.4kg 
 
Disassembles to package about 80 x 40 x 50cm.
 
First testing is very encouraging. Ride quality as good as previous (20 inch
wheel) design, steering better, maximum lateral cornering performance better
(10cm lower seat height). 
 
Next steps are a lot more testing, and construction of carrier, then testing
in fully loaded touring configuration.

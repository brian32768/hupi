Maybe Magura uses mineral oil to save money?  With it they can use rubber
seals, instead of Viton and silicon rubber seals which cost more, and
provide better durability.

Magura is a lower cost brake system?  Just curious.  If not, then they may
have higher margins.  No reason to be offended by that if they work well.

DOT 5 is OK with natural rubber parts:
http://www.clearcoproducts.com/pdf/specialty/NP-dot-5-brake-fluid.pdf
This would be the high cost leader I think.

Only DOT 3 and mineral oil are readily available in 55 gallon quantities.
 DOT > 3 is much less common.

This is the sort of first cut investigation a product design engineer will
do regarding every part in a machine.  For trikes you would need to extend
the investigation to encompass international suppilers, shipping, and
currency issues.

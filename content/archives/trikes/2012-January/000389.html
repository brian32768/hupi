My commentary is general regarding disc brakes.  Some of us including Fran
ride long tours where a basic understanding of some details can come in
handy in the hinterlands. Many of us do not have handy
trike savvy mechanics to rely on or the funds to pay them. Hopefully,
knowing that brake fluids can be be intermixed and that they all will work
safely together will save someone extra trouble when the local auto parts
store only has automotive brake fluid.  I can imagine knowing that olive
oil might work in a pinch being useful information.  (Actually a number
of vegetable oils have higher boiling points than mineral oil, thought they
may break down quickly, they would probably get you down off
a mountain safely and without damage to the parts.)

Rather than quash this thread, I would really like to know more about
Magura components being incompatible with normal brake fluid. Obviously, I
would never have assumed such a thing.  Sounds like crazy talk to me.

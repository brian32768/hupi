Hi Mike,

Erm, we weren't talking cars, we were talking turn of the millennium Hope cycle hydraulics that have been sat in a shed for the last half of that.

The same problem exists with some cycle hydraulic brakes - Hayes are the tricksy ones I've owned using hazardous fluids where as all other brands I've owned have had more 'user friendly' blood.

Either way, the advice holds - first line of attack is to let the local specialists do the job - they'll get the brakes cleaned and serviced more quickly than Fran or I will be able to do at home, with specialist parts such as replacement seals and the fluids & service kit with correct fittings, and all probably for little more than the cost of an eat-in snack as they do the work. Having chatted with Fran in the past I don't think brake servicing would be a job she'd actively want to take on herself. I do the same with the hydraulic stuff myself - every trike seems to have a different brand/model of brake and the local guy has the service kit to hand and the livelihood to maintain.

Rob

> Hi Rob,

> If you were to put mineral oil in a car brake system there would be
> no assurance that the components and seals would have the expected
> performance or life expectancy.
> 
> A designer of a modern braking system would most likely (to avoid liability
> issues) select materials to go with the glycol ether and polydimethylsiloxane
> that are typically used.  These should be OK if mixed together.  If you are
> still in the business of selling and servicing trikes, you might want to
> consider using approved brake fluids just to CYA.
> 
> Mineral oil was common in decades past; it is no longer considered a
> quality brake fluid.  There is also an additive package blended with DOT X
> fluids that extend their performance and life.
> 
> Food grade mineral oil will have no additives to prolong life, and all
> mineral oils are fragile in comparison to the common brake fluids when
> exposed to air and heat.  They quickly form polymeric sludges when abused.
>  FWIW, I am currently working with an apparatus using mineral oil at 400F,
> so the specifications are fresh in my mind.

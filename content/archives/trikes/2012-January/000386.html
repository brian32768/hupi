On Thu, 12 Jan 2012, Michael Ross wrote:

> If you were to put mineral oil in a car brake system there would be
> no assurance that the components and seals would have the expected
> performance or life expectancy.

Yes, but -- as you may have overlooked -- we don't drive no cars here ...

> A designer of a modern braking system would most likely (to avoid liability
> issues) select materials to go with the glycol ether and polydimethylsiloxane
> that are typically used.

No, not for bicycle use. A "designer" (??? I would prefer an engineer) of 
a "modern" (whatever that means) braking system should use something 
appropriate for the specific task.

> Mineral oil was common in decades past; it is no longer considered a
> quality brake fluid.

This is wrong. Mineral oil (or better "hydraulic fluid") is a very 
appropriate medium for hydraulic bicycle brakes. All hydraulic Magura 
brakes for example are bleeded with mineral oil and will be destroyed by 
car brake fluid.

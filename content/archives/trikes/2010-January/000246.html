Chris Heffel wrote:
> Hey guys,
> 
> I guess I am hard up for cycling time.........I decided to re-string my 
> seat fabric on my Greenspeed GTR.  It needs to be tightened, 
> straightened, and I need something to do.
> 
> I have been VERY happy with my Greenspeed overall, but I was 
> disappointed in how the seat fabric was attached.  The metal grommets 
> hit the horizontal seat frame pieces, and have ground gouges into the 
> paint/metal.  I noticed online that other GTR's have the fabric sliced 
> so that the grommets do not touch the metal frame.  This probably also 
> helps against the fabric sliding and slipping over time.
> 
> I cleaned and touched up the gouges last night with some of the touchup 
> paint that came with the trike (sweet) and am going to restring the seat.
> 
> How far is the fabric cut on your seat mesh?

Sounds like your seat is too small - there should be plenty of overlap 
and the brass grommets should be nowhere near any steel. You could whip 
up a new one on a sewing machine, or else slip in a bit of fabric to 
keep the metal bits apart.

More overlap means a stiffer seat, as there is less bungy cord to 
stretch. that way it won't fret on the paint as much.

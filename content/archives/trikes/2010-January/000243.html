Hi Bob,
 
Following your advice, I have been testing with plastic soft drink bottles.
They are working fabulously - the lack of a rubber internal dividing
membrane seems to make no difference. 
 
The damping is better able to be adjusted, as the opening into the soft
drink bottle is larger than the openings into the pressure accumulator I was
using. And of course, the bottles cost nothing and are 1/10th the weight of
the accumulators! Also, the lack of a need to bleed the system, use two
filling points, etc, is excellent. 
 
The 'wooshing squishes' of water shooting in and out of the bottles is
interesting, but that occurs audibly only on the largest of bumps. 
 
At least I now have a system I can extensively test. 
 
Thank you.
 
Regards,
 
Julian Edgar

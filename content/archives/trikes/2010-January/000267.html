I just refreshed the old 9sp rear mech on my Greenspeed GTX with a Shimano 105 Super Short. That is a Mountain Drive/9sp 9-30 cassette setup - a couple of teeth beyond the spec of the mech but it works fine and shifting is crisp. Backpedalling in the workshop in the largest ring feels rough as the top jockey is too close but when riding with the chain under load there is no problem.

Hope that helps...

>> As previously mentioned I have had problems with my derailleur dragging on
>> the ground when passing over rocks and through holes. (16 inch wheels.) Ian Sims of Greenspeed suggested fitting a short cage derailleur and a
>> Schlumf front drive, the latter reducing the necessity to take up so much
>> chain slack when running on the small front cog. I fitted a short cage derailleur but kept the front triple chainrings - and
>> found what perhaps lots of people already know... if you're careful in your
>> gear selection, you can use a 9 speed rear cluster and a three front
>> chainrings with a short derailleur. I just make sure that when I am on the
>> largest front ring, I don't change down much on the rear cogs before
>> dropping down a front ratio.  It's pretty easy to remember, especially when
>> the front cogs are right in front of you. All combinations of the smaller two front chain rings work with all
>> combinations of the rear gears - it's only when on the large front ring that
>> you need to take care. The shorter cage also seems to keep high speed gears tracking better -
>> perhaps the previous long cage derailleur was flexing sideways a bit with
>> the length of slack chain in front of it swinging from side to side?
>> I have improved the derailleur ground clearance by about 30mm.

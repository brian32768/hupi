Hi Jules,
If your wrench isn't used to recumbents he might not have seen the problem before - it is usually a problem of water collecting at the low point of a cable loop - either from rain, road spray or condensation. Stick bikes cable runs tend not to have these loops at the lowest point for moisture to accumulate and so they drain naturally. 
Sealed cables, either as Phil suggest using lube, or as I mentioned using PTFE liners, fixes the problem.

If you are going to the expense of the work I'd strongly recommend the lined cables but I suspect the tech used by each cable brand is pretty much the same... I think I paid GBP20 (US$30?) for my last set of Flak Jacket gear cables and GBP10 (US$15) for Nokon (a very special offer - they seem to be 4x dearer now) both of which I've been happy with.

Good luck!
Rob

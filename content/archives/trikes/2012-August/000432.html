Bob,
One aid is a pedal stick that connects to a pedal shaft and allows you to
spin the cranks while shifting and observing the derailleurs. I use one
with my WAW velomobile.   It looks like an animal control stick.

Its a 40" piece of 1/2" ID plastic pipe with 60"of parachute cord fed
through it. The pedal end  has a small hole in the side of the pipe for the
cord. The cord is knotted so it can't slip out. The other end of the pipe
has a saw kerf slot just large enough for the cord.  In use a pedal sized
loop of cord is created at the pedal end and slipped over the pedal to the
axle. At the other end of the stick, the slack is pulled out of the cord. A
strategically located knot is slipped into the cord slot to keep the cord
under tension.

The pipe can be any length to suit your application.

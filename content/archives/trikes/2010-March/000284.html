> Another opinion.  When I got my GTO I was still recovering from a herniated
> disc condition and found that a smooth as possible ride was needed.  I tried
> Schwalbe, Big Apple, 60mm and have really liked these tires.  Probably have
> 4000 miles on these and the center tread is gone, but like you say, I have
> just not wanted to spend the money to replace yet.  I have gotten a few
> flats, but in every case what I dug out of the treads would have gone
> through most tires.   I run these at a very low pressure ( I know Michael
> Ross thinks I am a little crazy for this).  I started using 40psi and about
> 1 year ago decided to change to 30psi to see what would happen. Can't tell a
> lot of change, but it does not seem to add perceptible rolling resistance --
> but no real testing has been done.  Schwalbe indicates that the recommended
> low pressure is 22psi.   These tires have a good grip -- it is even
> difficult to push the trike sideways in the garage to get it out of the way.
> 
> Middle of last year I had to start riding my mtn bike since I broke a rib in
> my back and the trike reclining position was too painful.  So decided to try
> the Big Apple on the mtn bike.  Worked quite well.  I really liked the quiet
> relative to knobby tires on asphalt.  I was running with 30psi.  I could
> even do easy single track, but would occasionally lose traction on roots and
> muddy areas where a knobby tire is really needed.

>> I have always felt that chance plays a large factor in the frequency of
>> flats.  I am sure many people change tire make and model because they get

>> is

>> data to make valid decisions.
>> 
>> Given that everything I say has to be filtered through the knowledge that
> I
>> may be confusing coincidence with good statistical reasoning.
>> 
>> I believe that the curbside wheel gets more flats because there is more
>> trash there than towards the crown of the road.
>> 
>> I think wet roads cause more flats.  The theory here (not my own, but I
>> keep
>> it under advisement) is that the water makes slicing cuts easier and glass
>> slivers can cause more damage before breaking up. This agrees with another
>> observation of mine that I can cut rubber with a blade more easily if I
>> have
>> water present - it does seem to lubricate the blade so it cuts more
>> smoothly
>> and quickly.
>> 
>> I have more flats in winter time.  I think the roads are dirtier for some
>> reason.
>> 
>> I think I get more flats on trikes than on two wheelers.  But with trikes
>> I
>> think it is harder to evade problems (not that I am very attentive).
>> 
>> How a tire is weighted makes a difference - I almost never flatted the
>> front
>> tires of my BikeEs which are VERY lightly loaded.
>> 
>> Really thin tires don't get much use on my bikes.  I like fat tires.  But,
>> I
>> like them to be soft and flexible.  I sometimes pump them up tight and
>> other
>> times not.
>> 
>> (Lot's of variables and differing conditions to be making comparisons,
> eh?)
>> 
>> Also, I am very non-reactive to flats in terms of giving up on tires and
>> changing brands.  And I tend to ride on the same sorts of roads and
>> conditions most of the time.  Even so the occurrence of flats seems to
>> oscillate for no apparent reason - sometimes more, sometimes less.
>> 
>> How large and high quality is my sample (Mike's flats as a predictor for
>> all
>> flats)?  I think I have had maybe a hundred in 10 years.    I remember a

>> rides

>> this
>> a topic that gets cogitated upon for hours on rides.

>> I like Comp Pools, Primo Comets, and Panaracer Pasela.  I would buy

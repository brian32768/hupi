Yes, I saw that as well, but you can twist statistics a lot. Of course
there would be no bike accidents if there were no bikes ...

makes even less sense to discourage transport by bike. Anyway, there might
be local considerations that we can't even imagine - which is exactly why
it's such a shame that the article doesn't give a reason that students are
not allowed to bike/walk.

Behind many of these policies is usually a couple of very specific
incidents that noone wants to see repeated, ever. Better disallow any risk

community (the school's "customers") demand(!) action.
Yes, my guess is a fatal (or near-fatal) accident involving a child on foot
or bike. But it's just a *guess*. Does anyone here *know*?

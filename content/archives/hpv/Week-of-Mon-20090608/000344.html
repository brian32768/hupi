> This is the Demo   I did for those at this strictly Human Power Event of 
> the drive I built for the Portland OHPV ePower event. I started out to just do 
> some boundary layer control using the fan, but when I tried to improve the 
> thrust to make me a bit more competitive I ended up with what you see in 
> Bill's pics. The theory is based on the work of Fabio Goldschmied at NACA using 
> an annular slot to pull the turbulent boundary layer in and redirect it out 
> the rear thus adding "negative drag"(pressure thrust) in place of some of 
> the form drag of a streamlined body.

Hi Paul,

I'm Scott McElhiney... at Portland on Saturday afternoon you strapped 
that contraption to my GTO trike and let me buzz around the infield a 
bit.  I was wondering how the E-assist races went on Monday.  How many 
miles did you end up doing? 

For everyone else... when strapped to my trike, it didn't make enough 
power to really get me rolling, but as soon as I pushed the pedals over 
once, it just sort of floated along gathering speed slowly.  I didn't 
pedal at all as I wanted to see if it was really pushing me along.  I 
slowly picked up speed even as cornering and changing directions.  I'd 
have been curious to see how fast it would have eventually have taken me 
on a flat straight track.  It may have been acting a bit as a tailbox 
even on my trike by sucking in that turbulent air behind the seat area 
and thrusting it out.  Pretty interesting concept, although I don't 
think it could be as efficient as something mechanically driving the wheels.

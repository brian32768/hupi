I'd point out that "cheapest" is not at all correct, it's the most expensive in the world dude!!! The entire peloton is on carbon, not steel ... where you been?

 

If you want cheap AND fast, change the geometry on the frame, move the downtube up like Boardman or Obree's bikes and it's 7% less drag than connected to the bottom-bracket.

 

Without that UCI rule, steel can compete by being not being penalized in the wind-tunnel by an arbitrary way to make a frame, has nothing to do with structure integrity, the design was outlawed explicitly because it was significantly less drag.

 

So, I totally disagree with the statement that the rules make it the cheapest when the absolute opposite is the truth.

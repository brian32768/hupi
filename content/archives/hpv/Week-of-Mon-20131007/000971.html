Here we have real winter so wet isn't often the problem. I prefer a trike with an extensive fairing over being trapped in a sweat box. http://mnhpva.org/ice/2005/pages/30.html
This way I'm dressed for the weather when I get off the bike. For rain, a simple cape 
turns it into a head out Velo and I have a helmet with a full visor.

My other winter ride is a highly modified '85 Trek (http://bikesmithdesign.com/MyBikes/snowmobile/winter3.jpg) with Winter Marathons, 700-35 in the front and 26" x 1.75 rear. It gets around great, even in thick, car churned "Mashed Potato" snow. 700-28 front/700-35 rear would be ideal but you work with what you have or can get.

I tried a 20x1.75 studded tire on an LWB but it just floated on the snow like it was hydroplaning. If I could get the WM or Kenda Klondike in a 451-28 I'd still be using an LWB 'bent in the winter. 
@ 67 y/o with osteoporosis it would be nice to be a little closer to the ground.

Barchetta huh? Been a long time since Ferrari's built anything that could be described as 'fun'.

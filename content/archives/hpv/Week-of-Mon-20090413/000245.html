Here's looking at you, Cornel. Eighteen people dying during a 9-year 
period, is a bit of a stretch for forming a conclusion.
 
For me, even small amounts of alcohol consumed before or during exercise 
reduce my physical ability to make a set of pedals move.  Even one weak 
beer can make the hills seem steeper and wind stronger than they really 
are. On the other hand a splash of "liquid cheering agent" after the 
last  ride of the day does okay by me. According to a recent published 
study the combination of moderate exercise with moderate alcohol 
consumption  can be a healthy choice.

Jane Østergaard Pedersen, Heitmann, Schnohr and Grønbæk. 2007. "The 
combined influence of leisure-time physical activity and weekly alcohol 
intake on fatal ischaemic heart disease and all-cause mortality" 
European Heart Journal. 
http://eurheartj.oxfordjournals.org/cgi/content/full/ehm574v1

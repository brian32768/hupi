John Tetz wrote: 
 
> Lets say the up right conditions are CdA 2.9 
> Cr .005, rider weight 170 lbs,  bike 17 lbs,
> drive train eff of 97%. To climb a 6% grade
> at 6 mph requires 159 watts. 
> 
> For the recumbent the CdA is 2.4 Crr .0055,
> same rider weight of 170 lbs, bike 30 lbs,
> drive train eff 94% (intermediate idler).
> The climb power is 170 watts.
 
Other than the typo of recumbent Crr .0055 (s/b 0.055 -- one reason why I exhort my students to include the leading zero in the "ones" position) I dispute two items: 
(1) (recumbent CdA)/(upright CdA) = 2.4/2.9 = 0.83 
considering the HUGE downhill speed advantage of a recumbent over an upright, I would expect this ratio to be no greater than 0.67 
(2) "drive train eff 94%" 
compared with the "drive train eff of 97%" for uprights, this means the intermediate idler converts 3% of its throughput to heat, which is unlikely. 1% or less is much more plausible, my estimate being 0.5% .

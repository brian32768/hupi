At 07:03 PM 2/28/2011, Jeff Wills wrote:
>That's somewhat old hat. Some tandems and single-speeds use a 
>chainring or sprocket placed between the two straight chain runs to 
>vary tension. This is called a "ghost chainring", per Sheldon Brown: 
>http://www.sheldonbrown.com/tandem-build.html . You can see a video 
>here: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YKmUoLPzDIc
>
>The only advantage the Roll-Ring has is that it's flexible and works 
>with different chainring/sprocket configurations. With a rigid 
>chainring, you need to have several different sizes available to 
>fine-tune tension.

Not so.  You fine tune the tension by moving the ghost ring closer to 
one or the other timing ring.  When the timing rings are the same 
size the chain is slackest when the ghost ring is midway between them.

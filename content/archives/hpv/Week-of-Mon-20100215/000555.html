Tom you have some great points. 

I would like to add some of my own. 
Because we have zero bike infrastructures I try to promote local alternate transportation for now. The 2 to 5 mile stuff for shopping and running errands. That can add up to 2500 to 3000 miles a year. Appreciable help for the environment and my health. 

And that's what most Europeans do - short haul local travel. I seldom saw a bike in between towns and cities on a couple of my European tours.  

Because I predominantly do local travel I don't get into the "athletic" performance area at all. At 78 those days are over. Nor do the Europeans get into athletic performance.  I do raise my heart rate, aerobic rate and get great exercise which helps lower my health care costs.  Average folks can handle this level of "performance."  

 And for help in climbing I have a just a little electric help - which adds only 4.2 pounds to the vehicle. 

>Street designers and transporation engineers are not cyclists, 
....and they don't ask cyclists for their inputs. 

>look at Europe and cycling, it's a heavy pro sport, 
....I try to stay as far away as possible from the words cycling and sport. Here in the US that's predominantly what bikes are used for. Mention the word bike to a lay person and the first thing that comes to mind is Sport, then Kids and Toys. I have heard this innumerable times - and written in news papers.  

When I go up town in my VM someone always stops to talk to me. They like my little car. This is a step  forward in consciousness for them - car equals transportation. And that's the way I use my VM. The concept Bike limits that consciousness change. And in truth a bike is seasonably and weather limited along with poor crash protection.  

I like the idea of us writing the manuals. Most manual writers aren't even aware of VMs.

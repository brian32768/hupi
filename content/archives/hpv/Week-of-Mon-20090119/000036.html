> Hey, I love watching the society of the peloton in action.  The peloton is
> beautiful,  exciting to watch, and efficient. But it does not represent any of > the ways

Well, a bit maybe! I do try to draft behind younger riders at times when 
commuting and certainly do so in group touring which would be too fast for me 
otherwise. I also get a almost totally free ride occasionally when I can "catch" 
a bus.


 > Let's not argue whether the recumbent rider
 > could be a member of the peloton society.

Let's! High recumbents are not much different from low uprights. But recumbents 
seem to get more benefit from uprights than vice versa.

...
> I believe that our recumbent technology with streamlining is important to the
> future of transportation.  It deserves a place at a big table.

Quite. But we have a long way to go. I only had one ride in a fully faired trike 
in rolling countryside on fast roads in strong winds. It was fast and 
exhilarating! But the same vehicle was next to useless where I live in suburbia: 
too slow and sweaty uphill and too fast downhill. Even tests with assisted 
vehicles were too sweaty for me. But I think this could be solved with active 
ventilation. See for example http://www.hupi.org/HPeJ/0002/0002.htm

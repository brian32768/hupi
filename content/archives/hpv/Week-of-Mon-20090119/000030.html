Stuart,

Agreed ... time trial bikes are different from the conventional road machine, but even the time trial bike has to conform to strict UCI regulation.

The operative word in one of your sentences are "bicycles tested".  My point was that this is precisely what the UCI is opposed to.  The UCI goal is the testing of rider performance not bicycle performance.  An unlimited (open class) time trial stage I never expect to come to pass.  It runs against the grain of what the UCI stands for.

In 1933, a Frenchman named Marcel Berthet rode a recumbent bike to a new speed record of 50km/h. A furor arose in the UCI.  After considerable debate, the UCI decided in 1934 to outlaw recumbent bicycles from competition in UCI sanctioned events.  I feel that it is unrealistic to expect a reversal of that decision anytime soon.

Jim McNamara

 Another overly complex answer to a question of resonance.

The ordinary pneumatic bicycle tire already is a shock wheel. This mechanism and the common piston types just extend the frequency range down lower the way that sub-woofers do for audio. Its real necessity is questionable. Any bump a bicycle encounters can be finessed by rider actions if it can be reacted to in time. However, the reduced amount of extra weight and  clearances it requires may be a slight improvement to other suspension mechanisms, and of course it may be useful for some aftermarket upgrades to existing bikes for which no other suspension is possible. 

My Lightning faired recumbent benefits greatly from just the small amount of shock reduction Tim Brummer adds with his very short throw simple front suspension and it doesn't require me to give up V-brakes on the rims.  This shock wheel could do much the same, but it would need disk brakes.  I suspect that the Shock wheel may also have tire/rim separation problems with any sort of large diameter high pressure tire. It probably is running fairly low in the video. Not so much with the folding bike in the other video whose wheel width to diameter ratio allows a stiffer lateral alignment to be had with the system. That wheel will not be jumping up onto any curbs, however.

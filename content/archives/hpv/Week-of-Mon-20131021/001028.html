Interesting concept, but I see a number of disadvantages compared to a 
traditional wheel and suspension:
* Requires redundant springs (not all working simultaneously)
* Heavier than traditional wheel
* Requires stiffer/stronger rim because of 3 or 4 attachment points to 
rim (rather than ~36 on standard spoked rim)
* Lateral compliance likely to compromise handling and steering
* Energy losses due to cyclic spring deflections even on smooth surfaces
* Expensive to manufacture

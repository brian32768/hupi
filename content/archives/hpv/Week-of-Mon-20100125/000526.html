Mark Stonich wrote:
> At  1/29/2010 08:31 PM -0600, Tom Sherman wrote:
>> What is the best current source for short cranks (140-150 mm)? I 
>> would be wanting something along the lines of 48/38/24T rings.
>
> Tom,
> When you go shorter you will be spinning faster.  So you can get by 
> with smaller large rings than you are using on 165-175s.   And most 
> recumbents start out geared too high for most riders.
Agreed. Much better to have low enough gears to spin up hills, than high 
gears which are not useful unless you have your feet behind a fairing, 
and then only useful for short time periods on downhills.

I used Sheldon's Gain Ratio calculator <http://sheldonbrown.com/gears/> 
which accounts for crank length to come up with the 48/38/24T for a 
559-mm drive wheel. Most manufacturers would put on a 52/42/30 which is 
too high for most riders.

Yeah, researching cutaway front ends like Windcheetah but for tubing, if you raise the downtube above the chainrings like a polo bike of the 80's it's almost 1-mph faster average speed by removing a huge source of turbulence where the dt meets the bb ... if that's done to cheap bikes it saves the average commuter calories per day, if you scale that it's a lot of food saved daily by simply moving the tube up, and the people get there a bit faster.
 
The frame doesn't need any other change to gain this advantage. I sent in a letter to the UCI to state that in their rules so non-racers would know that unless you race in UCI events there's no reason to go slow, no reply.

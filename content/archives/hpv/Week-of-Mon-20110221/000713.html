In this design 3hp was what I designed to, you may lose a full hp going up a hill with a load and a fluid drive, they aren't too great up hills that way, haven't done any math on this one but bet you can lose 30% as a worst-case. 
 
The trouble with the tiny engines is their high rpm's (many are 10k), a lower rpm tiny engine is more ideal to this purpose and could be designed, or maybe something is already out there. Designing to 1.5k/min is even hard, wheel rpms on a bike are pretty slow.
 
So a fluid drive solves the mismatch in rpms at the cost of heat from fluid friction in slip and cheap to make versus gearing, more expensive & efficient, don't think there are many other choices that are non-battery.

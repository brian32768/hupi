To keep the bike a nice ride, adding in an assist without powering the chain, no batteries, my sketches have a tiny RC model style engine of 3-4 hp, the shaft driving a cylindrical torque-converter to step down the high rpm's with the outside of that the drive-wheel to the tire for the assist, behind the bb is possible on many bikes, the seat-stays aren't very strong for this. The torque-converter means it'd add to your pedaling as mentioned.
 
That'd do the hills & all, light & small, tiny tank but needs to be muffled well and I want it running biodiesel so it needs a soot removal system in the exhaust, the soot melts ice in the Arctic. If an engine was designed specifically for this would be best, just seems a best combo of least energy used, get up the hill with a load or when a headwind is blowing hard and not alter the bike at all.

Oh yeah, you'll NEVER charge up an electric car in 10 seconds.  The  
best solution to that is what "Better Place" is doing.  Robotic swap  
of battery pack in a few minutes.

However, you will be able to pretty much do ALL your braking via Regen  
and nothing I mean NOTHING will ever match the acceleration of an  
electric car with these sort of batteries.  A Tesla does 0-60 in 3.9  
seconds with a bank of A123 batteries.  You could accelerate at the  
limit of adhesion  and with electric drive it is very easy to design  
control systems that will prevent you from engaging wheel spin.

FYI: DeWalt has a 48v portable power tool system on A123's ... charges  
up in 5 min.

As to these new batteries, my point was you could use a smaller setup  
and still absorb the energy from regen.

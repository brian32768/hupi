The formula for Kinetic Energy (KE = 0.5 * m * v *v) says the same as E 
= Fs when Vo = 0.0 m/s..

Little "t" was the center of attention. John Tetz suggested an example 
of looking at an eBike's change of velocity lasting  6 seconds (t') 
compared to 18 seconds (t").  The moves and pieces  were; F = m*a, a = 
(Vf - Vo)/t , dV = (Vf-Vo), and E = F*s which together allow showing 
time in the formula for Energy, as E = m * dV/t  * s.   Using the 
inputs; m' = m", s' = s", dV' = dV", t' = 6 seconds and t" = 18 seconds 
will give the result  E' = 3*E".  (see ref)

This perspective gives support to the following statement made by Tetz;

> Slow down that 0 to 60 in 6 seconds mentality to something like 18 
> seconds or higher and automatically the batteries could be smaller, 
> the motor smaller, drive train lighter, the whole vehicle lighter, 
> efficiency higher - now we are talking sensibly. We have adequate 
> technology now!!!!
No new technologies involved, just an application of patience and 
regarding one's Joules as valuable . <wink>

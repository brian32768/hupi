Chris,

I've performed real world tests with my 250 Watt Bionx with Lithium Ion batteries, but I do not have precise quantitative results.  If I only use the lowest level of assist and only on the last mile of my 6.7 mile ride to work and then use the regen setting of 2 or higher for the 4+ miles of downhill on the way home, I can ride every day without ever having to charge the battery.  In this mode, I only save about 5 minutes from my total commute time, but I am much more likely to ride knowing that I do not have to supply all the power to climb the 457 feet in the final mile to work.  Coming out of work, I can go 30 mph or better with the full regen braking on the big hill right by where I work.  On the other hills I can set the regen level to 2 and go better than 15 mph.  I do not know how the numbers in the diagnostic mode on the system correspond to standard units.  When it says my regen level is "20" or "40", I think it is just a number on a scale that Bionx set up.  The settings on the Bionx systems can vary.  My max regen is set to "45".  It can be set higher.  This is useless in flatter settings and might be overload on the system with my long 10% grade hill.  My system serves me well, but I live close to the foothills South West of Denver and ride up into the foothills to go to work.  I have a gross elevation gain of 1000 feet between home and work (a net elevation gain of 700 ft.).  For my situation, the regen works well.  I don't think it would work as well in flatter areas, but there you could go a lot further on a single charge anyway.

John

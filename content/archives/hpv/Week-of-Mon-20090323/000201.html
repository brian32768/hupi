> Thank you, Torben! I can see why he uses mechanical braking, as the 
> drive is one-sided only. As two hub-motors are rather expensive, as is a 
> differential or two complete drive trains, I am wondering about using a 
> rigid axis for both wheels connected to a single motor. This would 
> indeed improve straight-line dynamics. However a completely rigid 
> coupling to both wheels would give excessive tire scrub in turns and 
> maybe even introduce unfavourable shaking in turns. Therefore at least 
> one of the wheels should be attached with an adjustable clutch set at a 
> pretty high torque, but just able to give when turning, I guess around 
> 50 Nm (Newton-meters). If I built it myself I'd use conical steel axis 
> stubs coated with kevlar (glued-on fabric with little resin) and an 
> aluminium alloy hub drilled to the same taper, with a disc spring and 
> adjustable locknut on the outside.

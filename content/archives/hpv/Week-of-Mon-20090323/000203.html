Theo Schmidt wrote:
> I am wondering about using a rigid axis for both 
> wheels connected to a single motor. This would indeed improve straight-line 
> dynamics. However a completely rigid coupling to both wheels would give 
> excessive tire scrub in turns and maybe even introduce unfavourable shaking in 
> turns. Therefore at least one of the wheels should be attached with an 
> adjustable clutch set at a pretty high torque,
Theo,

Take a look at the chain routes in the Lightfoot Cycles power assist 
trailer. The picture is small, and may need to be viewed off line to 
catch any detail; http://www.lightfootcycles.com/powerpusher.php  It may 
be the case that Rod built a functional differential by using a 
jackshaft connected to the engine/motor in the shaft's center and 
connected at either end of the shaft to two standard bicycle drive 
wheels. This would allow power to both wheels while providing the 
independent clutch mechanism for each wheel as Al suggested.

YouTube has several video clips of people who have built single-wheel 
power assist trailers, which simplifies matters. This clip 
http://tinyurl.com/ce67qt goes to an example using an internal 
combustion engine.  You may wish to turn down your computer's speaker 
volume before viewing the video. <smile>

Cheers,

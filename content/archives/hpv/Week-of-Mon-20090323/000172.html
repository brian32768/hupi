I've wondered how to categorize all these bikes. I feel that trikes  
will always be too heavy and thus not viable VMs... : )  No, I'm just  
being funny. I see 2-wheelers as being part of the velomobile scene.  
But maybe that's not realistic? The challenge is "just" in finding a  
2wheeler that's easy to start and stop. Mine was easy! So I  
considered it a VM. As you say, the 2wheelers sure seem better able  
to beat the overweight penalty. If I want 30 lbs I think I need to go  
all-carbon and 2wheel (or maybe carbon and foam and you might  
suggest). But tipovers are a drag. ...Unless you have a totally easy  
escape hatch. And don't tip over into fast traffic... Well, that's  
why my vehicle was brightly colored! But I live in a low traffic  
area. If me, as a "weirdo" tipped over, locals would give me a break  
since there's no congestion. This brings up the question of LANDING  
GEAR. Can landing gear be made simple, reliable and light and thus  
better than adding a third wheel? At any rate, I see 2 wheelers being  
included in the VM equation. But it does bring up the definition  
problem. What's a VM? What about the many exposed bikers ('bent and  
upright alike) with cargo, lights, fenders and rainwear and studded  
tires in winter...they say "bring it on" and seem to quite clearly  
use their bikes as "mobiles"---they replace the car with the bike,  
they use the bike as a car. I've seen partly enclosed utility bents--- 
that even had roof-sections---with easy foot-down capability---which  
would seem to meet the standard of a VM. ??

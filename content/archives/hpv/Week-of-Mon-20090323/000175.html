Theo wrote;

>I don't agree. Over here we still have lots of "mopeds" with i.c. engines. 
...I fully understand and agree with your complaint. But this is true only because the mopeds are designed to be used continuously.  Think of how much trip pollution you will get on a truly assisted vehicle that your using the assist intermittently and only using one gallon of fuel 3.8 liters in 1300 miles, 2,000 km. 

>Here there is no need to reduce power the way John Tetz does 
.......If I don't use my assist it is so light it doesn't burden the rider. However if its 15 or 20 pounds the rider will not be willing to drag all that extra weight around so they will use it much more often. Designing for more continuous use automatically means it has to have a bigger battery, hefty motor/gear box  resulting in  high overall weight, and more continuous use. Sort of a negative feedback loop. 

The thing is the high efficiency of a bicycle and the low power of a human make an elegant combination. I try to maintain that elegance.

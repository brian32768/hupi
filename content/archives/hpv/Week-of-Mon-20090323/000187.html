For one of the early IHPVA Symposiums, Dr. Alan Abbot presented a design
that he built that was a somewhat encentified version of what John Tetz
did.  In the Abbot version he set it up so that the harder that you
peddled the more power you got!  Sort of a "strain gauge on the crank
connected to the throttle" control system.  He indicated that this
really encouraged the rider to exercise more (a primary goal for the
pediatrician).  His implementation was admittedly not very "clean" (IC
chainsaw engine of the 80's) as it was just intended to be a concept
platform.  Jack Lambie had created a "power assist trailer" that would
give the rider the ability to "unhook" for local flat rides, but when
the trip had hills, would give the rider an electric assist at the touch
of a button.  His goal was something that an older rider could handle,
and not have to customize the basic bike.

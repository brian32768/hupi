Great to see activity on the HPV list.  

I didn't want to see the discussion on acceleration from stop signs go by without pointing out that rubber might do the job. 

A 100 kg bike and rider accelerated to 15 mph = 30 m/s has kinetic energy = .5*100*30^2 = 45000 J

>From Bicycling Science, 1997, p 317, elastomer (rubber) holds 8 kJ/kg, so a bungee cord holding 45 kJ would weigh 5.5 kg = 12 lb. Wind it up coming to a stop, then reverse gearing and let it loose to accelerate. It couldn't get you up a hill, but such a device could help a lot in stop and go in flat country.  Seems to me it could be simpler and cheaper than an electrical system.

Wish I had the time and the mechanical aptitude to work on such a device...  Neither likely.

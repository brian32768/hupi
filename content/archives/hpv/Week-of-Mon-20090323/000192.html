>My faired Lightning F-40(modified) is to me a velomobile.
>I have doors on both sides for ease of entry. 
>It is open at the bottom so my 
feet can drop at stop signs easily, 
>I find that this system is a reasonable 
compromise for utility. 
>The material I have used for this fairing modification is very rugged 
compared to the sailcloth or spandex 
>I have ample room behind my seat for all the   groceries I normally buy, and 
that fits all inside the fairing so it doesn't slow me down

Paul, I can see from your list why you would classified your vehicle as a Velomobile. But wouldn't it better fit into a road streamliner classification? 
I think the main reason is you don't have to balance what is commonly been called a trike based Velomobile. Not having to balance to me has been the greatest benefit. It's sooooo much more relaxing when coming into a busy intersection and seeing a car coming - you simply put on the brakes and stop. None of this frantically getting the feet down in time. Another advantage is when crawling along at slow speeds such as 1 or 2 mph  - it's no problem at all to slow when climbing a steep hill or even stopping to rest and start up again.  
I also find that after running the VM for weeks at a time when I do eventually get on a bike I wobble a bit. It takes a bit of unconscious effort to balance it and kept it straight. A little bit of energy but its required all the time. I was not aware of this until riding a Velomobile. 

And during winter when riding over a patch of  black ice (specially at night) is again no big deal. I have suddenly gone down on my Orange Foam Shell a few times because of a small patch of ice or snow.

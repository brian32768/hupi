I agree on the general superiority of recumbency ... except when it comes to multimodality and bus bike racks. AFAICS, the double racks deployed by most transit providers are recumbent-hostile:

- the space between the slots into which the bike wheels sit is too narrow

- the front-wheel clamp expects at least a 26" wheel

> I found out long [ago] that I'm faster on long, hilly rides on a
> recumbent than an upright because on an upright my speed really
> drops once I'm having to go into pain management mode.

Agreed--human wrists and crotches have just not evolved for load bearing.

And regarding hilly rides: nothing quite compensates for a long climb like a high-speed descent :-) and I suspect that virtually anyone on any reasonably-well-designed recumbent can downhill faster than virtually anyone on an upright. I don't have the data (has anyone studied?) but I'd bet the difference between the population means is large relative to each population's variation.

On Tue, Oct 1, 2013 at 3:09 PM, Paul Gracey <pngracey at mac.com> wrote:

> I remain stubbornly recumbent. Yes I still have some upright bikes hanging
> in my garage that I occasionally get out, pump up and remind myself why I
> ride recumbent once again.
>
> It isn't my nature to follow the latest trend, never has been. While
> recumbent riders are significantly in decline along with the style of
> riding that they favor, I have not been influenced, partly because I have
> ridden with effective fairings for over 23 years now, and like the speed
> they give me. Let me tell of my latest acquisition. I purchased a recent
> Dahon Folder because it fits my Prius and allows for my 8 inch Newtonian
> telescope to come along on my road trips now that I am fully retired.
> Naturally this is all inside the car for economic, wind noise and
> pilfer-proofing reasons. At first I rode the Dahon as it was intended. Then
> I thought I'd do the Doug Milliken thing (Moulton with fairing) with it. I
> built a PETE head bubble blowing device, cobbled together  with a half
> round wastebasket attached to the front cargo boss of the Dahon and a body
> sock. This worked, sort of. But it was way too big, way too clumsy and I
> have never developed the ability to ride in a semi-tuck that
>   you racing types have spent years learning to endure.
>
> So back to the drawing boards (yes I STILL use one despite my computer
> skills) and a long desired conversion of the Dahon to a recumbent. It is
> still a work in progress, but I have made it workable so far. And the old
> Personal Peloton fairing I originally devised from a pair of Piper Cherokee
> Aircraft wheel pants has proven to be as aero-effective as it was when I
> had it on the Lightning P38. To retain the Dahon's folding capacity and its
> ability as an upright bike I used a Gates carbon fiber tandem timing belt
> to link the two separate crank/bottom bracket assemblies for oil-stainless
> fingers during conversion. The seat is from two different backpacking camp
> chairs and some minor structural additions.
>
> Limited to just the single Gates Belt length, the boom extension is not
> optimal as a recumbent for my leg length, and I don't anticipate any longer
> rides with this bike, but it seems about as useful in that configuration as
> some other manufactured recumbents I have tried over the years and has
> survived some bumpy roads I thought might be its undoing despite the fact
> that the welded M6 tapped boss provided by Dahon was only meant for a small
> basket as an attachment. I think that if I rode with clips, or had racer
> strong legs it might not hold up, but it did survive a 200M effort at the
> San Jose Velodrome early this year.

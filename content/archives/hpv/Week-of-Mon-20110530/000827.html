Why does the Quest 20 use 451 mm wheels instead of the much more  
common 406 mm wheel size? There aren't many 451 mm tyres available  
and they are hard to find in most bike shops and department stores.  
406 mm has become the standard for most recumbents and folding bikes  
using "twenty inch" wheels so there is lots of tyre selection and  
availability in this size.

Since the Quest has disc brakes it seems 406 mm wheels would be an  
easy retrofit. Would you sell the Quest without wheels to people who  
want the versatility of 406 mm wheels so they can put 406 mm wheels  
on right from the start and not have surplus 451 mm wheels? Better  
yet, how about just offering it as a factory option with 406 mm  
wheels and see which version sells better.

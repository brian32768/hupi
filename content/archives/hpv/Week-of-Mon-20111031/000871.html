> I'm leery of "pedicabs" which are large trailers (always without 
> their own brakes) whose gross, loaded weight far exceeds that of 
> the mountain bike and rider towing it by the seatpost. 
 
There was at one time a flourishing pedicab industry in Las Vegas, but it was virtually shut down seven years ago when they were prohibited from operating on or near the Strip, largely at the behest of the taxicab industry. I saw a lot of pedicabs when I moved here in 1999, but I don't recall seeing any trailers on the Strip. 
 
Because of the paucity of bicycling data in general, I doubt you'll have much success finding information about trailer safety. I can only recommend you ask on the Pedicab Forum (pedicabforum.com) and hope for a response. As an alternative, you may find useful data regarding trailer weight versus tow-vehicle weight ("towing capacity") at your state Department of Motor Vehicles, the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (nhtsa.gov), or the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (iihs.org). Good luck.
